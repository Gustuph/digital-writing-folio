# digital-writing-folio
A folio for my digital writing

My name is Angus, I'm a student of creative writing at RMIT. 
My writing takes a number of different forms. 
### Genre Fiction
These are the mediums that first spoke to me, they're the modes that I read as a child and what I often feel most comfortable in. Science Fiction and Fantasy are a lot of fun to come up with ideas for and allow for some really fun metaphors and extrapolations of ideas.
### Literary Fiction
I always think the idea of literary fiction is quite obnoxious, a way to define "serious" books as separate and distinguished from genre fiction, even if it just boils down to a grounded, contemporary setting that's explorative of ideas beyond the story. 
A lot of the work I'm most proud of is what might be best defined as literary fiction, in spite of how much I despise the term. 
### Non-Fiction
I also dabble in memoir-esque non-fiction, though that is a very new and unpolished area. 
While fiction demands at least some level of self-expression, I find that I can really speak my inner self when I'm writing non-fiction, and the freedom to explore and incorporate ideas and philosophies and topics that interest me outside of the initial piece of work is very enjoyable.
### Digital Writing
My current course of study has driven me here, however, to an exploration of digital writing. 
It's a pool I've not really dipped a toe in, so at the very least it will be interesting to dive into.

My writing process typically includes long sessions with white noise flooding my headphones, a keyboard at my fingertips and too many spelling mistakes to fix in a single sitting flagging on the screen before me as I go. 
When I'm in the brainstorm phases, I really enjoy writing just to see what comes out, but this is best done with pen and paper. 
I think this is largely because I like the physical sensation of crossing something out. 
It feels very emblematic of this stage of the process, striking out old thoughts and replacing them with new, more enticing ideas.
The idea generation phase is largely constrained to my notes app on my phone. 
Any time I think of something I could use later, it finds a place in there.
I'd hesitate to say much finds a home in the app, it's much more akin to a high schooler's locker than to a hotel where every idea gets a room and a keycard.
Notes are made in the wrong folder and never moved, they contain multiple unrelated ideas, they're not spell checked or reviewed, and some are lost to time, my forgetful inattentiveness, and my inability to conjure the dreadful amount of energy I would need to scroll through and find them.

As I mentioned previously, writing in the digital environment is very new to me, so I find myself a bit mired by trepidation, no matter how exciting the possibilities and ideas seem.
Alongside writing, one of my greatest hobbies has been gaming, so I've long been exposed to and enraptured by the way interactivity with a story can make it come to life.
It allows for immersion and buy-in that can be tough to find and achieve in more traditional print media.
